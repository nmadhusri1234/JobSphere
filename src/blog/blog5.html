Building an Impressive Project Portfolio

Creating an effective resume is the first step toward landing your dream job. Employers and applicant tracking systems (ATS) often filter candidates based on how well their resume is structured, formatted, and worded.

1. Use a Clear Structure

Organize your resume with consistent headings: Contact Info, Summary, Skills, Experience, and Education. Use bullet points for readability and ensure there’s white space for clarity.

2. Optimize for ATS

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems. To pass these filters, include job-relevant keywords and avoid fancy graphics or tables that may not parse correctly.

3. Highlight Achievements

Instead of listing job duties, focus on what you achieved in each role. Use action verbs and quantify your results. For example: “Increased customer satisfaction score by 25% in 6 months.”

4. Tailor for Each Role

Don’t use the same resume everywhere. Customize your resume for each job application by aligning your experience with the role’s requirements.

5. Keep It Concise

For most candidates, a one-page resume is sufficient. If you have 10+ years of experience, a two-page resume is acceptable, but ensure everything is relevant.

6. Include a Summary Section

A brief 2-3 sentence summary at the top should clearly explain who you are, your core skills, and your career goal.
