🚨 Fake Job Alert Tips

🔍 Common Red Flags

  • ❌ Asking for money for job confirmation, training, or registration.
  • 📩 Unofficial emails like job.hr@gmail.com instead of company domains.
  • 💬 Poor grammar or suspicious language in offer letters or messages.
  • ⚡ Offers without any formal interview process or technical rounds.
  • 📝 No official company website or LinkedIn presence.
  • 🔗 Links redirecting to strange or unrelated domains.
Legit companies never ask for payment during hiring.

🛡️ How to Verify a Job Offer

  • ✅ Check the company website’s careers page.
  • ✅ Cross-verify HR contact on LinkedIn or official email.
  • ✅ Ask for official domain email confirmation (e.g., hr@companyname.com).
  • ✅ Search for the offer or company name + "fake job" on Google or Glassdoor.
  • ✅ Contact the company via their official phone number/email.
Always ask for proper documentation before taking action.

🚫 Avoid These Scenarios

  • 💵 Paying any fee to get shortlisted.
  • 📥 Downloading attachments from unknown senders.
  • 📞 Sharing OTPs or personal bank information.
  • 🧾 Responding to interview calls from shady WhatsApp/Telegram groups.
  • 💼 Accepting job letters with no company logo or signature.
If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is!

📢 What to Do If You're Targeted

  • 📧 Report to cybercrime: https://cybercrime.gov.in
  • 📩 Email the company if their name is misused.
  • 📸 Save screenshots, emails, and all proofs.
  • 🚫 Block and report the sender on platforms.
